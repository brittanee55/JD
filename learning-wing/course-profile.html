Overview

What You Will Learn

Source Literacy 101 helps users slow down and evaluate information more carefully. It teaches how to identify what is documented, what is claimed, what is interpreted, and what still needs more evidence.

Skill

Critical thinking and evidence review.

Best For

Podcast listeners, students, researchers, and creators.

Outcome

Create safer summaries and stronger research notes.

Lessons

Course Lessons

01

Fact vs Claim

Learn the difference between something documented and something someone says.

02

Theory vs Speculation

Understand the difference between an interpretation and an unsupported possibility.

03

Primary vs Secondary Sources

Learn when to look for official records, direct documents, books, or journalism.

04

Disputed Interpretations

Learn how to handle historical topics where credible sources disagree.

05

Writing Safer Summaries

Practice language that is accurate, fair, and clear about uncertainty.

Skills

Skills You Build

Evidence Review

Learn how to ask, “What supports this?” before accepting a claim.

Careful Language

Use phrases like “claimed,” “documented,” “disputed,” and “requires review.”

Research Notes

Organize sources, questions, summaries, and open threads.

Final Project

Create a Source-Aware Episode Summary

Final Course Project

Choose one podcast episode and write a summary that separates documented facts, guest claims, disputed interpretations, speculation, and open questions.

  1. Choose one episode.
  2. Write a short summary in your own words.
  3. Identify three claims that need source review.
  4. Find at least two credible sources.
  5. Rewrite the summary using careful language.
