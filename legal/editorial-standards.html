Editorial Standards & Disclaimer

Human Knowledge Atlas is designed as an educational research and reflection tool. It organizes conversations, sources, timelines, locations, and ideas while clearly separating documented facts, claims, interpretations, disputed topics, and open questions.

Standard 01

Fact, Claim, Theory, Speculation

The Atlas should label information carefully. A documented fact is different from a guest claim, a historical interpretation, a theory, or speculation.

Standard 02

Living People & Institutions

Content about real people, organizations, governments, companies, or institutions should be handled with extra care. Avoid presenting unsupported allegations as facts.

Standard 03

Educational Use

The Atlas is for education, research organization, reflection, and media literacy. It is not legal, medical, financial, psychological, or professional advice.

Standard 04

Copyright Respect

Book pages, episode pages, and newsletter issues should summarize in original words. Avoid copying long passages from copyrighted books, transcripts, articles, or paid content.

Standard 05

Corrections & Source Review

When better sources are found, entries should be updated. Corrections should be welcomed, reviewed, and incorporated when they improve accuracy or context.
