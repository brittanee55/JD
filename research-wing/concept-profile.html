Definition

What Are Intelligence Agencies?

Intelligence agencies are organizations that collect, analyze, protect, and sometimes act on information related to national security, foreign policy, military strategy, cyber threats, terrorism, and geopolitical risk.

Simple Meaning

They help governments understand hidden or complex threats.

Main Function

Collect information, analyze risks, and inform decision-makers.

Atlas Use

Connect episodes to history, public records, books, and open questions.

Why It Matters

Why This Concept Matters

Intelligence agencies affect diplomacy, war, cybersecurity, counterterrorism, surveillance, public trust, and government accountability. Understanding them helps users separate documented history from claims, theories, speculation, and disputed interpretation.

Security

Agencies are often justified as tools for national protection and risk analysis.

Secrecy

Their work can involve classified operations that are difficult for the public to evaluate.

Oversight

Democratic societies often debate how secrecy should be balanced with accountability.
